I like things that feel right

I like starting with messy ideas and slowly turning them into something that feels… calm. Logical. Right. Starting with a blank canvas, figuring out what it could be, and shaping until it fits. But give me an existing solution that feels slightly off, and I’ll happily roll up my sleeves and start working. Figma has become my favorite place to play, test and shape ideas into things you can actually click and feel.

Through my bachelor’s degree in Interactive Design, I’ve gained experience across the full process of creating digital products. From user research and understanding needs, to concept development, prototyping, user testing and final delivery. I’ve worked in cross-disciplinary teams, used agile methods, and learned how to plan, structure and execute projects in collaboration with both peers and external stakeholders. My studies have also given me a solid technical understanding of how web applications are built, how data is structured, and how frontend and backend connect. This allows me to communicate effectively with developers and design within real technical constraints.

In my bachelor project, I worked closely with an established company to develop a digital solution based on real business needs. The project gave me hands-on experience in client collaboration, requirement clarification, project management and delivery — as well as adapting to new tools and technologies along the way. After completing my Bachelor's degree, I’ve worked as a photographer. It has trained my eye for detail, composition and atmosphere, but also for people. I bring this observational sensitivity into design, especially in user testing and research.
