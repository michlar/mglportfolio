Byåa Games

An interactive learning app designed to spark curiosity about local history and encourage children to explore nature.

Team: Group project (8 members)

Course: Agile Project

Focus: Interaction design, educational gameplay, mobile UI

Tools: Figma

Project overview

This project was developed in collaboration with Rælingen municipality. Our team was tasked with creating a digital experience that could teach primary school children about the local history of an area called Byåa, known for its historic mill ruins and cultural trail.

The goal was to design an app that combines outdoor exploration, storytelling and play — making history feel engaging and accessible for younger audiences.

Team and process

The team consisted of eight students across interaction design, front-end development and game development. Working in an agile setup, we collaborated closely with Rælingen municipality through workshops, feedback sessions and iterative prototyping.

Designing for children

The primary target group was primary school children. This influenced both interaction patterns and tone of content — keeping tasks simple, feedback clear and navigation highly visual.

To validate design decisions, we conducted user testing at schools in Rælingen. Observing children using the prototype helped us refine game mechanics, simplify navigation and adjust language to better fit their understanding.

The concept

The app guides children along the cultural trail, presenting historical locations as interactive story points. At each stop, children unlock short stories, images and playful challenges connected to the site.

Learning through play

To make history engaging, we designed several mini-games connected to the trail locations. The games encouraged collaboration, problem-solving and discovery rather than passive reading.

A long-term vision for the concept was to allow multiple real-time games to be created along the trail, motivating children to return, play together and spend more time outdoors.

Interaction flow

A short onboarding tutorial introduces key features, helping first-time users understand the app before starting the trail.

The interface was designed to feel location-driven and easy to follow. Children navigate using a trail map, select nearby activity points and receive short, focused content designed for outdoor use. Clear visual navigation ensures they always understand what to do next.

Visual system & UI design

The UI combines friendly typography, strong color contrast and playful iconography to create an interface that feels inviting. Consistent components and clear hierarchy support predictable interaction across the experience.

Future potential: AR experience

A planned extension of the concept was an augmented reality feature. By scanning QR codes placed at selected ruins, children could point their camera toward the site and see reconstructions of how the original buildings once looked — connecting physical exploration with digital storytelling.
